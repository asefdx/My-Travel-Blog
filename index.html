Exploring the Beauty of Cox's Bazar

Cox’s Bazar is one of the most breathtaking places I’ve ever visited. Known as the world’s longest natural sea beach, it stretches over 120 kilometers along the southeastern coast of Bangladesh. From the moment I arrived, I was captivated by the endless stretch of golden sand, the rhythmic sound of the waves, and the peaceful vibe that surrounds the beach town. My trip started early in the morning when I arrived at the main beach area. The sea breeze was refreshing, and the sun was just rising over the horizon, painting the sky with beautiful hues of orange and pink. I spent the morning walking along the beach, collecting seashells and taking in the beauty of the Bay of Bengal. In the afternoon, I visited Himchari National Park, just a short drive away. The view from the top of the Himchari hill was absolutely stunning — I could see the endless coastline meeting the horizon. Later, I went to Inani Beach, known for its coral stones and cleaner, quieter environment. It’s the perfect spot to relax away from the crowd. What made the trip more special was the delicious seafood. I tried freshly grilled fish at a local beachside restaurant, and it was one of the best meals I’ve had. The hospitality of the local people also made me feel very welcome. In the evening, the beach transformed into a magical place. The sunset at Cox’s Bazar is something everyone should experience at least once. The sky turned shades of purple and red, and the reflection on the water was mesmerizing.

Travel Tips:

  • Visit during the winter season (November-February) for the best weather
  • try local seafood especially the fried pomfret and lobster
  • Bring sunscreen and stay hydrated.
  • Don’t miss visiting St. Martin’s Island if you have extra time!
